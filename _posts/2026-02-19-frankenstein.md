---
title: "Frankenstein"
author: "Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley"
rating: 4
---

This book being one of the most-read classics definitely did not disappoint. I like the way the narrative structure follows letters being sent between the sailor and his loved one. Throughout the book I felt so much pity for the creature, as he initially had good intentions and hoped to be accepted by society. Even after proving himself to be docile, due to his sheer ugliness he was shunned by the family whom he had observed from a distance and learnt English from. If I'm not wrong, the book is set somewhere in Switzerland, so I recognised some of the landmarks mentioned, like the city of Geneva. I think the book touches on deeper points than I can fully comprehend, so all I can say is that it made me feel pity for the creature and contemplate how much appearances matter in life.

If I'm being honest, the biggest thing that I learned was that Frankenstein is not the name of the monster, but the doctor who made it.
