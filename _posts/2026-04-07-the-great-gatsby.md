A shimmering portrait of the Jazz Age that never quite lets you forget the emptiness underneath.

Fitzgerald's prose does most of the heavy lifting here. The sentences are so perfectly constructed that you almost miss how sad everything is. Gatsby himself is more symbol than person — but that's the point. He's the American Dream turned inside out, all green light and no substance.

Nick as narrator is an interesting choice. He claims to withhold judgment, then judges everyone relentlessly. Whether that's intentional irony or Fitzgerald losing control of his own character is a debate worth having.

The book is short, which works in its favor. It doesn't overstay its welcome. You finish it in an afternoon and spend the rest of the week thinking about it.
