A quiet, devastating book about what it means to love someone you can never fully understand.

Klara is an Artificial Friend — a solar-powered robot bought as a companion for a sick teenager. The brilliance is in the narration. Klara observes everything with precise, slightly alien attention. She misreads social cues, builds superstitions around the Sun, and loves with a devotion that feels both mechanical and deeply human.

Ishiguro restrains himself beautifully. The dystopian world-building is all implication — genetic editing, social stratification, obsolescence — but he never explains more than Klara would notice. The result is a story that feels intimate even as it gestures at enormous questions about consciousness and sacrifice.

The ending stayed with me for days.
