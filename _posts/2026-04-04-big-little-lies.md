---
title: "Big Little Lies"
author: "Liane Moriarty"
rating: 4
---

This book feels like it was written for mothers, in the way that it explores the hardships of motherhood so deeply. The way the characters are portrayed is so well done — every character feels three-dimensional, and it made me care for each and every single one of them. The male characters, on the other hand, are very two-dimensional, and I don't know if this was an intentional decision by the author. The plot twist was pretty crazy as well. I didn't expect who it was that died. The way the book foreshadows the death is very deliberate, through the countdown — "four weeks before trivia night" — and the periodic insertion of a detective's investigation transcript is so cool.
