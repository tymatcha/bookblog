The most fun I've had reading a book in a long time. Pure problem-solving joy wrapped in a surprisingly emotional story.

Ryland Grace wakes up alone on a spaceship with no memory of how he got there. What follows is a cascade of science puzzles — each one solved with the kind of methodical optimism that made The Martian work so well. But where this book surpasses its predecessor is the relationship at its center, which I won't spoil.

Weir's writing is functional rather than literary, and that's fine. The sentences exist to deliver ideas, and the ideas are genuinely great. A few of the flashback sequences drag, but once the main storyline hits its stride, it's hard to put down.

The ending is earned and satisfying. Not every book needs to be ambiguous.
